* Optimal choice


** Rational choice (I)

- The consumer chooses, among all affordable baskets, the one that
  provides him with the highest level of well-being:
  #+begin_export latex
  \[
    \max_{\displaystyle\{X^*, Y^*\}} U(X, Y)
    \stext{subject to\ } M \geq P_{X} X + P_{Y} Y
  \]
  #+end_export


** Rational choice (II)

#+MATS: figcol file:fig-07_1004-oc01-*.pdf 0.4

- The opportunity set comprises all the baskets that the consumer can
  afford.

** Rational choice (III)

#+MATS: figcol file:fig-07_1004-oc02-*.pdf 0.4

- Any basket on $\overline{BC}$ is preferred to $A$ if the
  non-satiation assumption is fulfilled.

** Rational choice (and IV)

#+MATS: figcol file:fig-07_1004-oc03-*.pdf 0.4

- The optimal basket is on the budget line if the non-satiation
  assumption is met.




* Interior solutions


** Interior solutions

- *Interior solution*: the optimal basket $(X^*, Y^*)$ contains some
  units of each good.


** Tangency condition (I)

#+MATS: figcol file:fig-07_1004-eq01-*.pdf 0.4

- At bundle $A$: $\RMS > P_X/P_Y$.



** Tangency condition (II)

#+MATS: figcol file:fig-07_1004-eq01a-*.pdf 0.4

- The consumer can get a higher level of utility if he increases
  the consumption of $X$.


** Tangency condition (III)

#+MATS: figcol file:fig-07_1004-eq02-*.pdf 0.4

- At bundle $B$: $\RMS < P_X/P_Y$.


** Tangency condition (IV)

#+MATS: figcol file:fig-07_1004-eq02a-*.pdf 0.4

- The consumer can get a higher level of utility by decreasing
  the consumption of $X$.


** Tangency condition (and V)

#+MATS: figcol file:fig-07_1004-eq03-*.pdf 0.4

- At bundle $E$: $\RMS = P_X/P_Y$.

- The consumer cannot increase his utility by varying the
  consumption of $X$.


** Optimum conditions

- Interior solutions ($X^*, Y^* > 0$):

  + Budget line: $M = P_{X} X + P_{Y} Y$.

  + Tangency condition: $\RMS = P_X/P_Y$.


** Example (I)

- Utility function: $U(X, Y) = X Y$.

- Prices: $P_X = \price{2}$; $P_Y = \price{2}$.

- Monetary income: $M = \money{70}$.


** Example (and II)

#+MATS: figcol file:fig-07_1004-ej01-*.pdf 0.55

- $X^* = 17.5; Y^*=17.5$.


* Corner solutions


** Corner solutions (I)

- In a *corner solution*, the optimal consumption level for some goods
  is $0$.

** Corner solutions (II)

#+MATS: figcol file:fig-07_1004-eq10-*.pdf 0.4

- At every bundle on the budget line: $\RMS < P_X/P_Y$.

- The consumer buys $Y^*=M/P_Y$ units of good $Y$ and $0$
  units of good $X$.


** Corner solutions (and III)

#+MATS: figcol file:fig-07_1004-eq11-*.pdf 0.4

- At every bundle on the budget line: $\RMS > P_X/P_Y$.

- The consumer buys $X^*=M/P_X$ units of good $X$ and $0$
  units of good $Y$.


** Optimum conditions

- Corner solution when only $X$ is bought:

  + $X^* = M/P_X, Y^* = 0$

  + $\RMS > P_X/P_Y$

- Corner solution when only $Y$ is bought:

  + $X^* = 0, Y^* = M/P_Y$

  + $\RMS < P_X/P_Y$


** Example (I)

- Utility function: $U(X, Y) = (X + 50) Y$.

- Prices: $P_X = \price{2}$; $P_Y = \price{2}$.

- Monetary income: $M = \money{70}$.


** Example (and II)

#+MATS: figcol file:fig-07_1004-ej02-*.pdf 0.55

- $X^* = 0; Y^*=35$.
