* The optimum choice


** Rational choice (I)

- The consumer chooses, among all affordable baskets, the one that
  provides him with the highest level of well-being:
  #+begin_export latex
  \begin{gather*}
    \max_{\displaystyle\{X^*, Y^*\}} U(X, Y) \\
    \text{subject to\ } M \geq P_{X} X + P_{Y} Y
  \end{gather*}
  #+end_export


** Rational choice (and II)

- If the non-satiation assumption is fulfilled, the optimal basket is
  on the budget line:
  #+begin_export latex
  \begin{gather*}
    \max_{\displaystyle\{X^*, Y^*\}} U(X, Y) \\
    \text{subject to\ } M = P_{X} X + P_{Y} Y
  \end{gather*}
  #+end_export




* Interior solutions


** Interior solutions

- *Interior solution*: the optimal basket $(X^*, Y^*)$ contains some
  units of each good:
  #+begin_export latex
  \[ X^* >0; \quad Y^* > 0. \]
  #+end_export


** Tangency condition (I)

#+MATS: figcol fig-2C_1004-eq01-*.pdf 0.45

- At point $A$: $\RMS > P_X/P_Y$.

- The consumer's valuation of an additional unit of good $X$ is higher
  than the relative price of $X$.


** Tangency condition (II)

#+MATS: figcol fig-2C_1004-eq01a-*.pdf 0.45

- The consumer can get a higher level of utility if he increases
  the consumption of $X$.


** Tangency condition (III)

#+MATS: figcol fig-2C_1004-eq02-*.pdf 0.45

- At point $B$: $\RMS < P_X/P_Y$.

- The consumer's valuation of an additional unit of good $X$ is lower
  than the relative price of $X$.


** Tangency condition (IV)

#+MATS: figcol fig-2C_1004-eq02a-*.pdf 0.45

- The consumer can get a higher level of utility by decreasing
  the consumption of $X$.


** Tangency condition (and V)

#+MATS: figcol fig-2C_1004-eq03-*.pdf 0.45

- At point $E$: $\RMS = P_X/P_Y$.

- The consumer's valuation of an additional unit of good $X$ is equal
  to the relative price of $X$.

- The consumer cannot increase his utility by varying the
  consumption of $X$.


** Optimum conditions

- Interior solutions ($X^*, Y^* > 0$):

  + Budget line:
    #+begin_export latex
      \[ M = P_{X} X + P_{Y} Y \]
    #+end_export

  + Tangency condition:
    #+begin_export latex
    \[ \RMS = P_X/P_Y \]
    #+end_export


** Example (I)

- Utility function: $U(X, Y) = X Y$.

- Prices: $P_X = 2$ € per unit; $P_Y = 2$ € per unit.

- Monetary income: $M = 70$ €.


** Example (and II)

#+MATS: figcol fig-2C_1004-ej01-*.pdf 0.55

- Interior solution:
  #+begin_export latex
  \[ X^* = 17.5; Y^*=17.5. \]
  #+end_export


* Corner solutions


** Corner solutions (I)

- In a *corner solution*, the optimal consumption level for some goods
  is $0$:
  #+begin_export latex
  \[ X^* = M/P_X, Y^* = 0;\quad\text{or}\quad X^* = 0, Y^* = M/P_Y. \]
  #+end_export


** Corner solutions (II)

#+MATS: figcol fig-2C_1004-eq10-*.pdf 0.45

- At every point on the budget line: $\RMS < P_X/P_Y$.

- The consumer buys $Y^*=M/P_Y$ units of good $Y$ and $0$
  units of good $X$.


** Corner solutions (and III)

#+MATS: figcol fig-2C_1004-eq11-*.pdf 0.45

- At every point on the budget line: $\RMS > P_X/P_Y$.

- The consumer buys $X^*=M/P_X$ units of good $X$ and $0$
  units of good $Y$.


** Optimum conditions

- Only $X$:

  + $X^* = M/P_X, Y^* = 0$

  + $\RMS > P_X/P_Y$

- Only $Y$

  + $X^* = 0, Y^* = M/P_Y$

  + $\RMS < P_X/P_Y$


** Example (I)

- Utility function: $U(X, Y) = (X + 50) Y$.

- Prices: $P_X = 2$ € per unit; $P_Y = 2$ € per unit.

- Monetary income: $M = 70$ €.


** Example (and II)

#+MATS: figcol fig-2C_1004-ej02-*.pdf 0.55

- Corner solution:
  #+begin_export latex
  \[ X^* = 0; Y^*=35. \]
  #+end_export
